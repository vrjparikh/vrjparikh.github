Electro Pneumatic Quick Shifting for FSAE Vehicles

Department of Mechanical Engineering
Indian Institute of Technology, India

The project aims to develop a seamless Electro Pneumatic Shifting System for FSAE cars. I developed the electronic circuit controlling this system using ATMega 16 and Arduino. It has been designed so as to support on-site tuning as well as launch control. I also designed a custom two stage Air preperation unit to increase the efficiency of this mechanism. It costs one-tenth of the commercially available systems. Currently modelling the air flow as a thermo-viscous flow to find out a mathematical model for the amount of air used per shft.

Mentor: Prof. A R Mohanty, Professor, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Kharagpur

Report: Electro-Pneumatic Shifting for K3. (Report in Progress)

Eccentric Differential Mounts for K4

Aerial Robotics Kharagpur
Centre for Excellence in Robotics, IIT Kharagpur

I am attempting to make a simple, strong yet hassle free mechanism for Securing the Differential i it's place as well as acting as a chain tensioning mechanism for a formula SAE car, K4, the season 17-18 car of TeamKART. Designed these on SOlidWorks, optimized on Hyper Works after analysis on ANSYS. The previous system weighed 4.5 kgs, the currently designed system by me would weigh under 2 kgs, with the same safety factor

Guide : Prof. Dhananjay Kumar Srivastava, Mechanincal Engineering Department, IIT Kharagpur

Electronic CUT Shifting with Launch Control

Kharagpur Automobile Racing Team
Mechanical Department, IIT Kharagpur

Iam developing an inhouse Electronic shifting system for FSAE vehicles. I aim at making this an inexpensive yet very robust kit. Will be using an Electronic Solenoid Actuator with 4 cm stroke and 200N max force, with comparatively low loading time. Developing the circuit for the same to be compatible with MoTeC RCU to implement CUT shifting and launch cotrol. Expecting to complete this by February 2017..

Guide : Prof. Prof. A R Mohanty, Mechanical Engineering Department, IIT Kharagpur

    Steering System for I Bike, A low Cost Autonomous Bicycle

    LBS Hall IIT Kharagpur
    General Championships, IIT Kharagpur

    The project aims to develop a low cost mechanically balanced autonomous bicycle to solve last mile transport problem. I woorked on the steering system of the bicycle. Developed a mechanical clutch nut system for switching between manual and autonomous mode. Also used Sprocket and chain drive system to couple the steering with a DC stepper motor which acted like a sevo. Feedback was taken from an IR based sensor locaed at the baseline of the bike.

    Report Low Cost Autonomous Control and Navigation of a Mechanically Balanced Bicycle with Dual Mode of LocomotionLink

    Synthesis of a Chebyshev Platigrade Machine

    Term Project
    Department of Mechanical Engineering

    I synthesized a model for the famous Chebyshev Plantigrade Machine which can convert rotational moion to linear motion of desired magnitude. This can be used to make legged robot cotrol easire, where much DOF is not required. This mechanism was then used to make a machine to walk on sand.

    Guide : Prof. K. Bhattacharya and Prof. A, Dasgupta, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Kharagpur

    Self Balancing Robot

    IEEE Winter Workshop, IIT Kharagpur

    The project proposed a two wheeled robot using 8-bit micro-controllers which can balance itslef and then move around. The complete mechanical deisgn for the IMU position was performed and optimised for low cost embedded devices.

    I developed and tested the PID loop used for provding stabilizing velocity to the wheels. Data source was IMU-6050 and the controlled parameter was the DC motor with a feedback system

    Report: IEEE Workshop: Self Balancing Robot

    Corridor Following using SONAR

    Technology Robotix Society
    IIT Kharagpur, IN

    The project aimed to develop a low cost robust lane following system to be used in robotics competitions

    I developed an algorithm for ensuring that the robot always stays in the center of the lane. Also implemented an algorithm to ensure 90 degree turns without the use of IMU or feedback from motor. Controlled speed of motor without using Hall effect encoder, using ultrasound map data.

    Obstacle Avoidance And Control On Autonomous Bicycle

    LBS Hall of Residence, IIT Kharagpur

    Created Depth Map using SONAR, in order to locate the obstacles in the way of the bicycle and then wrote an algorithm to avoid the obstacle and then rejoin the original path in order to reach the destination. Did triangulation using SONAR, and then avoided obstacles. Due to hardware restrictions had to switch to a simpler model finally, aided by an onboard camera. Odeometry was performed using hub motor for locomotion.

    This project won the KPIT Sparkle 2015
